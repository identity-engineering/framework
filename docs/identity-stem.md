# Identity Stem / Schwerachse

The **Identity Stem** is the innermost directed axis of identity. It runs through spacetime and is the geometric core around which everything else forms and curves.

We model it as a **Schwerachse** (principal axis / spin axis):  
Substance rotates around this axis. Habits act as tangential forces. Stability arises from rotation with minimal wobble — the classic gyroscope effect.

The Stem is not a static point. It is a thick strand composed of many fibers that describe the core self (ICH) across three temporal domains.

---

## 1. Past — State Differential

**What it is**  
The observed derivative of the state:  
`δS = dS/dt` (or discrete `ΔS = S(t+Δt) − S(t)`)

This is the causal chain of actual changes from the past up to the present moment.

**Function**  
Maximizes **Sameness-as-Trajectory**.  
Recognizability after change does not come from the current snapshot (which is copyable), but from the transparent history of how the identity has moved.  
“I hardly recognize you anymore” is already the phenomenon; AI only makes it central.

**Engineering task**  
Make the State Differential transparent and owned.  
The past is not a fixed story to defend, but a verifiable causal chain that authenticates the entity even after radical change.

---

## 2. Present — State

**What it is**  
`S(t)` — the high-dimensional state vector at the current moment (competencies, relationships, inner states, positions, resources, etc.).

**Function**  
The present is only the **current cross-section** of the Stem.  
It is necessary as the meeting point of past trajectory and future gradient, but it is never the full definition of identity.  
State ≠ Identity.

**Engineering task**  
Keep the present clear and uncluttered so that both the incoming State Differential and the outgoing Vision Gradient can be seen without distortion.

---

## 3. Future — Vision Gradient

**What it is**  
`V ≈ ∇𝒞`  
where `𝒞` is causal entropy (the measure of future freedom of action).

**The Gradient, simply explained**  
- It shows, at every point of a mathematical landscape, the exact direction of the **steepest ascent**.  
- It is not a number, but an arrow (vector): direction + steepness.  
- It is local and short-sighted: it leads step by step to the nearest hill (local maximum).  
- In AI, the reversed gradient (gradient descent) is the reliable tool that minimizes error and trains models.

In Identity Engineering, **Vision is the intended gradient of causal entropy**.  
It points in the direction in configuration space where future freedom of action increases most steeply.

**Function**  
Maximizes Causal Entropy.  
Vision is not the derivative of identity; it is the *desired future derivative* — the direction we deliberately choose to open the largest set of future possibilities.

**Engineering task**  
Sharpen the Vision Gradient. Make the direction of steepest ascent of freedom clear, strong, and owned.

---

## Integration — The Full Stem

Identity (the complete Schwerachse) is the **integrated Worldline** that holds both forces in productive tension:

- Backward: State Differential → Sameness-as-Trajectory  
- Forward: Vision Gradient → Causal Entropy

Formally:

```
I = arg max_γ [ ∫_{now}^{future} V(γ(t)) · dγ  +  consistency term with past State Differential ]
```

The **Integral of the Vision** is the line integral of the gradient along the trajectory.  
It equals the cumulative gain in future freedom of action realized by that path.

Identity Engineering works on exactly two primary levers:

1. Transparency and ownership of the **State Differential** (make the past visible and owned).
2. Clarity and force of the **Vision Gradient** (define the direction of steepest ascent of freedom).

The result is a Stem with high rotational stability (little wobble) and simultaneously high causal entropy (open future).

---

## Why this matters in the AI era

Static snapshots of identity become cheap and copyable.  
What remains authentic is the **signed history of differentials** that starts from a unique human origin and continues through freedom-maximizing updates.  
Observation of change itself constitutes ongoing identity.

The Identity Stem is therefore both the personal core and the foundation for future-proof, non-copyable identity systems.
